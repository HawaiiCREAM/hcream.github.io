# The failings of the current way my department creates collaboration
Collaboration is good. Collaboration is academia is even better. My department is investing effort and funding into creating colloquiums and seminars that see little attendees. How is this failing?



Facility of academics would be interested in maintaining a space for research to cross over one another. Colloquiums and seminars, henceforth referred to *advanced meetings* are an excellent way to create spaces for collaboration on research, but faces two flaws:

  1. Advanced meetings for specialized topics intimidates potentially interested students by creating a formal environment of expectations.
  2. Researchers are terrible marketers; hence the only attendees to advanced meetings are those that are already interested in the specialization.

By creating an aggressively formal environment that only caters to those already interested, advanced meetings fail to create interdisciplinary students.

# The purpose of a tea time
I preface this topic with the following disclaimer: this is not the catch-all solution to collaboration---but rather a step towards being better.

The most difficult part of collaboration is breaking the human barrier of contact. Many students, especially in mathematics, are afraid of the unfamiliar.
